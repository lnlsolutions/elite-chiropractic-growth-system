Patient Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are answers to some of the most common questions we receive from patients.

Do I need a referral to schedule an appointment?

Most patients can contact our office directly to request care. If your insurance requires a referral, our team can help explain the next steps.

Can you help after a car accident?

Yes. We commonly help patients experiencing neck pain, back pain, headaches, whiplash symptoms, stiffness, and mobility limitations following an auto accident.

What is whiplash?

Whiplash is a neck injury that can occur when the head is suddenly forced forward and backward. Symptoms may include neck pain, headaches, stiffness, dizziness, and limited range of motion.

Do you treat sports injuries?

Yes. We work with athletes and active individuals seeking support for injury recovery, mobility improvement, rehabilitation, and return-to-activity goals.

What conditions do chiropractors commonly help with?

Many patients seek chiropractic care for back pain, neck pain, headaches, injury recovery, mobility limitations, sports injuries, and general wellness support.

Do you offer physical therapy?

Yes. Physical therapy and rehabilitation services may be incorporated into care plans depending on the patient's condition and recovery goals.

How long is a typical appointment?

Appointment length varies depending on the reason for the visit, evaluation needs, and treatment plan. Our office can provide additional details when scheduling.

What should I bring to my first appointment?

Bring a valid ID, insurance information if applicable, and any documentation related to your injury or condition that may help our team better understand your situation.

How soon should I seek care after a car accident?

Even if symptoms seem minor, it is generally recommended to be evaluated as soon as possible after an accident because some injuries may not become noticeable immediately.

Do you work with athletes?

Yes. Dr. Naiel Salameh's sports medicine background allows the office to support athletes and active individuals working toward recovery and performance goals.

Do you treat back pain and neck pain?

Yes. Back pain and neck pain are among the most common reasons patients seek care at our office.

How do I request an appointment?

You can submit a request through the website, call the office directly, or contact the team using the information provided on our contact page.
